Understanding the difference between fitness, which focuses on physical training and the development of strength, flexibility, endurance, and body functionality, and the concept of wellness, which is centered on mental balance and the promotion of a healthy and harmonious lifestyle.

Engaging in physical activity is essential for physical and mental well-being. It helps maintain a healthy lifestyle, reduces stress, and lowers the risk of diseases. The benefits of sports primarily include the prevention of cardiovascular, metabolic, and neoplastic diseases, reducing the risk of heart attacks, hypertension, and strokes. It also contributes to preventing Alzheimer's disease. Moreover, physical exercise controls body weight, reduces adipose tissue, manages cholesterol and blood sugar levels, and improves posture, reducing musculoskeletal damage.

Besides the physical benefits, sports have significant advantages for mental well-being. It reduces stress, anxiety, and depression, improves sleep regulation, and enhances concentration during work and study. It acts as a natural antidepressant thanks to the release of endorphins during physical activity, promoting relaxation and boosting self-esteem.

Endorphins, produced by the brain, play a key role in psychophysical well-being. They act as neurotransmitters, alleviating pain, reducing stress, and inducing a sense of euphoria and well-being.

Sports also have significant social value, promoting social interaction, helping to make new friends, and feeling closer to others. It reduces loneliness and allows individuals to develop strong social relationships.

From an economic perspective, preventive sports activities have a positive impact on national healthcare services. Preventing diseases means avoiding hospitalizations, the use of medications, and other healthcare services. By staying healthy, absenteeism from work is reduced, contributing to business productivity.
